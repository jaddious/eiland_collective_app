Eiland: our neologism which borrows the Dutch term for islands (eiland) with the English plural suffix “-s”.

Moat (“gracht” in Dutch): A deep, wide ditch surrounding a castle, fort, or town, typically filled with water and intended as a defense mechanism against external attacks.

Mound (“motte” in Dutch): An artificially supra-elevated piece of land, in the context of the moated homesteads it defines the land surrounded by water. The elevation was usually created with the ground dug out during the creation of the moat.

Homestead: From old english, a house and outbuildings, from old English “hāmstede”, a settlement.

Farmstead: A farm and its buildings designated to both housing, farming and agricultural production.

Settlement: A place, typically one which has previously been uninhabited, where people establish a community.

GIS: Geographic Information System.

Wetland: Land consisting of marshes or swamps; saturated land.

Waterscape: A landscape in which an expanse of water is a dominant feature.

Stead: The place or role that someone or something should have or fill (used in referring to a substitute).

ROI: Region of Interest. Geographic extent in which moated homesteads are believed to be a recurring typology.

Morphogenesis: The origin and development of morphological characteristics. In urban planning it refers to the historical and geographical conditions behind the development of specific urban forms.

Typology: A classification according to general type. In urban planning it refers to the study of urban and architectural types, usually in vernacular architecture.

SWF: Southwest Flanders.

OSM: OpenStreetMap an open-source, free geographic database of the world, it provides various layers of geographic information, maps and routing information.
